# Qualification

Prior to placing products on the market, they need to be assessed whether they incorporate a medical purpose and meet the definition of a medical device as set out in MDR Article 2. Any product that meets the definition shall be considered a medical device and shall comply with MDR.

When qualifying the Software, MDCG 2019-11 is used for guidance on segregation of the modules of the software that incorporates the medical purpose. These modules are then qualified as Medical Device Software (MDSW) according to procedures set out in the guidance document. Any products utilizing software modules that qualify as Medical Device Software shall comply with the requirements in MDR.

Accessories intended to be used in combination with a Medical Device Software, even though they do not have a medical purpose itself, shall comply with the requirements in MDR.

The qualification for each device is documented in a regulatory conformity plan that form a part of the Device Regulatory File as applicable.

# Classification

Medical devices are classified as specified in MDR Article 51. The classification is carried out using the classification rules set out in MDR Annex VIII. The medical devices will be assigned one of the following classes: I, IIa, IIb, III. All of which are associated with different conformity assessment procedures as defined below.

The classification for each medical device is documented in a regulatory conformity plan that form a part of the Device Regulatory File.

# Conformity assessment procedure

The conformity assessment shall be planned and carried out according to MDR Article 52 and Article 53-56 as applicable. The chosen conformity assessment procedure shall be documented as part of the Medical Device Technical Documentation.

# Equivalence

Equivalence with similar devices placed on the market and the criteria for equivalence shall be determined and documented. Retrievable clinical data of equivalent devices (if any) shall be used in the clinical evaluation to support the clinical data. If the clinical evaluation is supported by clinical data obtained from equivalent devices this must be stated in each device clinical evaluation report (CER). The assessment and use of equivalence shall be kept as part of the Device Clinical FIle and form part of the Medical Device Technical Documentation.

To claim equivalence the following product characteristics must be considered and based on proper scientific justifications:

-   Technical
-   Biological
-   Clinical

# Identification and Traceability

Identification and traceability of medical devices shall be implemented in accordance with MDR Chapter III. This includes the use of UDI and registration in EUDAMED. The means for identification and traceability shall be kept as specifications in the Medical Device Technical Documentation.

# Change Management

Change management of medical devices shall be controlled and documented. All changes to the product must be assessed concerning its significance and impact on regulatory compliance.

Records of change management shall be kept as part of the Device Product File and form a part of the Medical Device Technical Documentation.

When implementing changes to the Medical Devices were Notified Bodied are involved in the conformity assessment procedure, any significant changes made to the medical device shall be communicated to the appointed Notified Body. The Notified Body shall have documented procedures and contractual arrangements in place that clarify how the reporting shall be done.

Standards used for each type of medical device are listed in the product GSPR-Checklist.

Currently there is no common specifications that applies to the products.

Applicable MDCG endorsed documents are used for guidance.

# General Safety and Performance Requirements (GSPR)

Medical devices shall comply to the requirements set out in MDR Annex I prior to placing them on the market. Demonstration of conformity with requirements shall include a clinical evaluation in accordance with MDR Article 61. Demonstration of conformity shall be recorded and kept as part of the Device Regulatory File and form part of the Medical Device Technical Documentation.

Applicable GSPR shall be identified and documented for each medical device. Non applicable requirements must be justified. Standards and/or methods used to demonstrate compliance with each applicable GSPR shall be specified and serves as input for planning of verification. Fulfillment of each applicable GSPR shall be supported with evidential documentation in a manner that demonstrate compliance.

# External testing

Based on the product characteristics externa testing may be required. Such testing could be for electrical safety, EMC and biocompatibility for instance. A suitable test house should be identified, and testing planned and scheduled.

Test reports from external testing shall be kept as part of the Medical Device Technical Documentation.

# Technical Documentation

For each medical device, a Technical Documentation shall be drawn up and be maintained. The Technical Documentation shall be presented in a clear, organized, readily searchable and unambiguous manner and provide information to allow conformity assessment with applicable requirements of EU 2017/745 MDR and to demonstrate fulfilment of elements listed in Annex II and Annex III.

The Technical Documentation shall be divided in the following parts:
- Device Product File
- Device Usability Engineering File
- Device Risk Management File
- Device Clinical File
- Device Cybersecurity File
- Device Post Market Surveillance File
- Device Verification and Validation File
- Device Regulatory File
