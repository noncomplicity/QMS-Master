### 5. Applicable Regulatory Requirements

The product and organization must comply with the following regulatory and standard-based requirements:

- MDR (EU) 2017/745 – Articles 5–61 and Annexes I–XVI
    
- ISO 13485: Quality Management Systems for Medical Devices
    
- ISO 14971: Application of Risk Management to Medical Devices
    
- IEC 62304: Software Life Cycle Processes
    
- IEC 62366-1: Usability Engineering for Medical Devices
    
- ISO 14155: Clinical Investigations (if applicable)
    

These requirements form the basis for process implementation, documentation, and verification activities.

---

### 6. Clinical Evidence Strategy

Clinical evidence will demonstrate compliance with the MDR’s General Safety and Performance Requirements (Annex I). The strategy includes:

- A systematic literature review to identify existing clinical data.
    
- An equivalence assessment where applicable.
    
- A clinical evaluation report (CER) documenting the evidence.
    
- If required, planning and conducting a clinical performance study following ISO 14155.
    

The need for clinical investigation will be evaluated based on risk class, novelty, and data availability.

---

### 7. Risk Management and Benefit-Risk Determination

Risk management will be conducted in accordance with ISO 14971 throughout the product lifecycle. Key elements include:

- Risk analysis and control during design and development.
    
- Residual risk evaluation and benefit-risk determination.
    
- Integration of post-market surveillance (PMS) data into continuous risk assessment.
    

The benefit-risk balance will be reviewed regularly to ensure ongoing compliance.

---

### 8. Quality Management System

The organization’s QMS, compliant with ISO 13485, will support all regulatory activities. It will cover:

- Management responsibility and resource allocation.
    
- Design and development controls for medical device software.
    
- Risk management integration.
    
- Supplier management.
    
- Internal audits and continuous improvement.
    
- Complaint handling, vigilance, and PMS.
    

If QMS certification is not yet obtained, a roadmap will be established for implementation and certification aligned with the regulatory timeline.

---

### 9. Technical Documentation and Traceability

Technical documentation will be compiled and maintained per MDR Annexes II and III, providing evidence of compliance. It includes:

- Product description and intended use.
    
- Design and development documentation.
    
- Risk management file.
    
- Verification and validation reports (including software testing).
    
- Clinical evaluation documentation.
    
- PMS plan and results.
    

A traceability matrix will link regulatory requirements to design inputs, verification evidence, and risk controls.

---

### 10. Post-Market Surveillance and Vigilance

A PMS system will be established to collect, analyze, and act upon feedback from product use. Activities include:

- Regular PMS data collection and analysis.
    
- Trend reporting and incident evaluation.
    
- Periodic Safety Update Reports (PSUR) where applicable.
    
- Integration of PMS data into risk management and clinical evaluation updates.
    

Vigilance procedures will ensure timely reporting of serious incidents and field safety corrective actions (FSCA) to the competent authorities.

---

### 11. Regulatory Interactions and Timelines

Key milestones and responsibilities are as follows:

- Regulatory strategy finalization – Regulatory Affairs Lead
    
- Classification confirmation – Regulatory Affairs and Clinical Lead
    
- Notified Body engagement – Quality/Regulatory Manager
    
- QMS implementation and certification – Quality Manager
    
- Clinical evaluation completion – Clinical Lead
    
- Technical documentation completion – Documentation Manager
    
- CE marking – Authorized Representative/Regulatory Affairs
    

A detailed project timeline will be developed to align regulatory activities with development milestones and product launch goals.
